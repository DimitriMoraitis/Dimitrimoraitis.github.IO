Q: What are the differences between a traditional IRA, and a Roth IRA?

A: A very common question among all demographics. In a traditional IRA which is abbreviated for Individual retirement account you are investing in a mutual fund that is ran by an individual like me. In that mutual fund we invest in primarily stocks. However the real difference is with a traditional IRA you are not taxed until you take your money out of the account. The benefit to that is you'll have more money compounded each time which can give you more profits. The other option is a ROTH IRA. With a ROTH IRA you are taxed every time you are given interest however when you decide to take your money out you are free of tax. This option can be beneficial if you think taxes in the future will be high.
